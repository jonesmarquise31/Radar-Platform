# The Career Chessboard (3C Framework)

**Date:** May 3, 2026

## What It Is

A tactical positioning methodology for senior IT professionals. Built around three operating principles that govern how a professional engages with the modern hiring system, the modern employer, and the modern market. The 3C framework is one of two operating layers underneath Radar's diagnostic engine. The other is the Abundance Mindset Reframe (AMR), documented separately in this folder.

## The Three Cs

1. **CEO of Your Own Career.** Operate the career as a business, not as a job sequence. The professional is the executive of a single-person enterprise. Compensation is revenue. Skill investment is R&D. Visibility is marketing. Network is distribution. The framing forces decisions about time, attention, and positioning to be made the way an operator would make them — against an explicit objective rather than against the path of least resistance.

2. **Consultant Mindset.** Sell outcomes, not effort. Hours worked, tickets closed, and tools known are inputs. The hiring system listens for outputs: business problems solved, risk reduced, dollars saved or earned, scope owned end-to-end. A professional who describes their work in input language is read as a contributor. A professional who describes their work in outcome language is read as an operator. The translation is structural, not cosmetic.

3. **Control the Narrative.** Own the story before the system reads its own version. LinkedIn, the resume, the recruiter call, the first interview question — each is a surface where the professional gets to define what is being measured. Letting the system pattern-match without intervention produces the system's default read. Operating the surface deliberately produces the read the professional needs.

## When It Applies

The 3C framework underpins how Radar's diagnostic engine reads positioning signals. Submissions are scored against the operator-tier expression of each principle — does the professional speak in outcome language, do they own their visibility, do they treat the career as a business — and clusters are assigned accordingly.

Buyers in the platform's full engagement tier receive applied versions of the framework calibrated to their specific archetype. The diagnostic surfaces which of the three principles is currently the weakest signal in the professional's positioning, and the engagement closes that gap.

## What's Public, What's Not

The framework's three principles, their intent, and the operator-tier framing are public. This document is the public version.

The applied versions — how each principle scores, how the scoring maps to archetype clusters, how the archetype determines which principle gets prioritized in the engagement, the exercises and templates that translate the principles into concrete moves — stay private to full-engagement buyers and to the platform's diagnostic engine. The platform does not publish its scoring. It publishes only what the framework is for.
